Credit Risk Models

An effective Machine Learning Model for Customer Credit Risk Classification must address two important issues:

  1. The imbalance in the proportion of good and bad risk customer records available for training the model.

  2. The asymmetry in the business cost of a False Negative (i.e., the incorrect classification of a bad risk as good) and a False Positive (i.e., the incorrect classification of a good risk as bad).

The class imbalance problem results in machine learning models that are biased to predict the majority (i.e., good) class. To avoid this bias, one of the following preprocessing strategies must be applied:

  • Over-sample the bad credit class records in the training set
  • Under-sampling the good credit class records in the training set
  • Synthesise new bad credit class records

The asymmetric business cost of incorrect predictions can be controlled by adjusting the threshold for credit approval to ensure that:

  • bad risk classifications are more likely (resulting in fewer defaults)
  • the reduction number of good risk loans is minimised

There is an inevitable trade-off between minimising the False Positive Rate and the False Negative of the model. This tradeoff is controlled by the chosen threshold and will depend on both the business model (e.g. many low profit, or fewer but higher profit loans) and the market place (e.g. historic default rates of the region).
